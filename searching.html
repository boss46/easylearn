A fundamental operation to a great many computational tasks is searching: retrieving some particular piece or pieces of information from large amount of previously stored information. Normally we think of the information as divided up into records, each record having a key for use in searching. The goal of the search is to find all records with keys matching a given search key. The purpose of the search is usually to access information within the record (not merely the key) for processing.


Read more

Applications of searching are widespread, and involve a variety of different operations. For example, a bank needs to keep track of all its customers’ account balances and to search through them to check various types of transactions. An airline reservation system has similar demands, in some ways, but most of the data is rather short-lived.
